PORK KATSUDON

Ingredients

  • 2 center-cut boneless pork chops, pounded down to 1 centimeter thick
  • Salt,
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cups dashi soup stock/li>
  • Oil
  • 1 cup panko
  • 5 large eggs, beaten, divideD
  • All-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 4 cups Japanese steamed rice

    • Recipe instructions (taken from the book)

    1. Gather the ingredients.AND Season the pounded pork chops with salt and pepper.
    2. Dust with a light, even coating of flour.
    3. In one shallow bowl, beat 1 of the eggs. Put the panko into another shallow bowl.
    4. Add a thin, even layer of oil to a cast-iron pan or skillet over medium heat. The oil is ready when you drop a panko breadcrumb into it and it sizzles.
    5. Dip the flour-dusted pork into the egg to coat both sides.
    6. Transfer the pork to the panko and press it evenly into the meat to get a good coating.
    7. Carefully lay the pork chops in the hot oil and cook for 5 to 6 minutes on one side, until golden brown.
    8. Flip and cook the other side for another 5 to 6 minutes, or until browned, crispy, and cooked through.
    9. Drain on a plate lined with a paper towel.
    10. Slice your tonkatsu into pieces.
    11. Put the dashi soup stock in a pan and heat on medium heat.
    12. Add the soy sauce, mirin, and sugar to the soup and bring to a boil. Remove from the heat.
    13. To cook 1 serving of katsudon, put 1/4 of the soup and 1/4 of the sliced onion in a small skillet. Simmer for a few minutes on medium heat.
    14. Add 1 serving of tonkatsu pieces (half of 1 pork cutlet) to the pan and simmer on low heat for a few minutes.
    15. Beat another one of the eggs in a bowl. Bring the soup to a boil and pour the egg over the tonkatsu and onion.
    16. Turn the heat down to low and cover with a lid. Cook until the egg has set and remove it from the heat. The egg should be cooked through.
    17. Serve by placing 1 serving of steamed rice in a large rice bowl. Top with the simmered tonkatsu on top of the rice. Repeat to make 3 more servings.
